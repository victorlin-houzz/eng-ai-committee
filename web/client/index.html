⚖ HOW IT WORKS
USING THE APP
1
UPLOAD YOUR DESIGN DOC
Click the upload zone on the right or drag & drop a file. Supported formats: PDF, DOCX, Markdown, TXT, ZIP. ZIP files are unpacked and supported files are merged into the editor; images are shown in the image strip.
2
EDIT BEFORE REVIEW (OPTIONAL)
The doc opens in a live Markdown editor. Make any last-minute edits. The review will use whatever text is in the editor at the time you click Start Review.
3
START REVIEW
Click "▶ Start Review". Watch the agents in the meeting room think, debate, and reach a verdict in real time. The whole pipeline takes 1–3 minutes depending on doc length.
4
SAVE & EXPORT
Click "💾 Save" to archive the review. Then "📄 Export Doc" to download the final document as DOCX or PDF with all embedded images restored.
THE 5-STAGE REVIEW PIPELINE
STAGE 1
STRUCTURAL
CHECK
A fast pre-screen checks that the doc has the required sections (problem statement, solution, metrics, rollback plan). Missing sections are flagged immediately before the expensive LLM calls begin.
STAGE 2
SPECIALIST
REVIEW
Three specialist agents run in parallel, each examining the doc through a different lens: Security & Compliance, Architecture & Infrastructure, and Product & Operations. Each surfaces findings with severity levels (Low → Critical).
STAGE 3
DEDUPLICATION
All findings from the three specialists are merged and deduplicated. Overlapping or near-duplicate issues are collapsed so the debate focuses on unique concerns only.
STAGE 4
SKEPTIC
DEBATE
The Skeptic agent challenges each finding: "Is this actually a real problem, or a false alarm?" Specialists defend their findings. The Skeptic rates each rebuttal as Convincing or Unconvincing. Unconvincing findings are dropped. Multiple debate rounds are supported.
STAGE 5
JUDGE
VERDICT
The Judge reviews all surviving findings and issues one of three verdicts: Pass (ship it), Revise (address issues and resubmit), or Reject (fundamental problems — do not proceed). A confidence score and revision memo are included.
THE AGENTS
SECURITY & COMPLIANCE
Reviews auth, data handling, secrets management, PII exposure, dependency vulnerabilities, and regulatory compliance (SOC2, GDPR). Flags anything that could create a security incident or compliance violation.
ARCHITECTURE & INFRA
Evaluates system design: scalability, single points of failure, data consistency, service boundaries, infrastructure costs, deployment complexity, and observability (metrics, logging, alerting).
PRODUCT & OPS
Assesses product-market fit, success metrics, rollback plan, phased rollout strategy, on-call readiness, support burden, and whether the doc clearly communicates the "why" to stakeholders.
SKEPTIC
The devil's advocate. Challenges every finding from the specialists and forces them to defend their position. Filters out over-engineered concerns, false alarms, and nitpicks — keeping only findings that truly matter.
JUDGE
The final decision-maker. Weighs all surviving findings holistically and issues the committee verdict. Also available for post-review chat to discuss findings and suggest specific doc improvements.
VERDICTS
PASS — The design is approved. No blocking issues remain. You may proceed to implementation.
REVISE — The design has addressable issues. Update the doc per the revision memo and resubmit for another review cycle.
REJECT — The design has fundamental problems that require a rethink. Address the core issues before resubmitting.
TIPS
BEST RESULTS — Include a clear problem statement, proposed solution, success metrics, failure modes, rollback plan, and infra cost estimate in your doc.
DEBATE ROUNDS — "1 round" of debate is usually enough. Use "2 rounds" for contentious docs where findings may need deeper scrutiny.
LOG TAB — The 📋 Log tab shows the full pipeline timeline: every stage, every agent action, and every finding in real time.
SAVE FIRST — Click 💾 Save before exporting to ensure the archive captures your latest edits. Unsaved changes are only in memory.
